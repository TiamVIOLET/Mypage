## 1. I think about who should see the first shot

This probably comes from being influenced by anime. When I make a page, I naturally ask what the user should see first. Is it the title, a patch of light, or a quiet space where the mood can settle? That thought feels very close to arranging shots.

## 2. Motion is not a trick, it is breathing

The motion I like is usually quiet. Instead of large dramatic transitions, I prefer something lighter and softer, like air moving. That makes the page feel like an emotional scene rather than a forced presentation.

## 3. Anime mood and frontend can belong together

Some people treat anime mood as visual style and frontend as implementation. For me, they are already connected. CSS can draw atmosphere, interaction can write rhythm, and a page can feel like a gentle opening sequence.

## 4. Maybe that is why I like frontend so much

Frontend is not only about building features. It lets me mix in the light, colors, framing, and emotions I like. Every finished page feels like completing one more storyboard frame of my own.
