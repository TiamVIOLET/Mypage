## 1. I am not very good at introducing myself directly

If someone suddenly asks me to formally say who I am and what I like, I will probably freeze. It is not that I have no thoughts. I am just slow to warm up, and I am not used to opening myself all at once.

## 2. But a page can say things I cannot say out loud

When I put my favorite colors, scenes, and ways of expression into a page, it slowly becomes something that feels very much like me. Even if I do not say a lot, people can still sense my personality from those details.

## 3. So a personal homepage is expression, not a resume

I do not want it to become a table that only lists information. I want it to feel like a small space of my own: quiet, blue, a little anime-like, and a little slow in the way an introvert is slow.

## 4. If you remember a little of Asa from here, that is enough

It does not have to be intense or loud. If someone finishes browsing and thinks, “this person seems to like blue, anime, careful making, and maybe feels a little cute,” then the page was worth writing.
