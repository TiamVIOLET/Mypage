## 1. I picture a scene before a feature

Whenever I start making a page, the first thing in my head is usually not layout. It is a scene: the wind before a train arrives, or the first streetlight turning on under a blue evening sky. For me, the emotion of those anime shots appears before the functional brief.

## 2. Blue has always felt special

I really like blue, especially the kind that feels close to night air instead of bright decoration. It reminds me of after-school streets, music in my headphones, and the feeling that the day is almost over but my emotions are not finished yet.

## 3. I want pages to feel a little like anime

I do not want a page to only place information in a straight line. I prefer it to feel like a small animated moment: atmosphere first, then content, then details opening slowly. Even a small card should feel memorable, like one frame in a storyboard.

## 4. Maybe this is also how I express myself

I am not a very outgoing person, so a lot of my need to express things flows into pages. The blue colors, night scenery, trains, wind, and quiet glow all feel a little like me.
